# Code of Conduct 📜

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the StudySwap community pledge to make participation in our project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### ✅ Examples of positive behavior:

- Being respectful and kind to other contributors
- Giving and accepting constructive feedback gracefully
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members

### ❌ Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, insults, or derogatory comments
- Personal or political attacks
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Trolling or deliberately disruptive behavior
- Any conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — GitHub repository, issues, pull requests, and any other community spaces associated with StudySwap.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by contacting the project team via Telegram: [@damiiiira](https://t.me/damiiiira)

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality regarding the reporter.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Project maintainers will follow these steps:

**1. Warning**
A private written warning for minor violations with clarification of the violation.

**2. Temporary Ban**
A temporary ban from any interaction or public communication with the community.

**3. Permanent Ban**
A permanent ban for severe or repeated violations of community standards.
